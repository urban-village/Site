Family Academy Model


“We decided to leave Burma because of the violence, but we decided to come to the United States because we wanted our kids to be educated here.” -Naw Dah

What is F.A.M.?

The Family Academy Model (or F.A.M.) is a cohort model program designed to enhance home-to-classroom engagement through the empowerment of parents and the support of students. In partnership with Saint Paul Public Schools, The Urban Village has adapted curriculum from the PIQE and designed it specifically for the Karen community. We believe that parent engagement plays a huge role in the success of students at school.

Our Family Academy Model walks alongside Karen parents in better understanding an education system that is complex for anyone to understand completely, but especially for someone who isn’t familiar with it. Do you know what FAFSA, GPA, CIS, AP, and ELL all stand for? No? Now imagine reading that in a different language? Now imagine what grasping all those concepts when you’ve never been exposed to them before? Following the tested and proven curriculum of the PIQE, we intend to equip and empower parents to better engage in their child’s education.

As parents receive training and experience mutual learning with volunteers, so to do their children. Our Family Academy Model is not just geared for parents, but for the entire family! The students enrolled in our program receive academic support and mentorship from our volunteers in the same space and environment as their parents. We hope to facilitate a family environment where parents and students alike are engaging each other within the context of educational success.
